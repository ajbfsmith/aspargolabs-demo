If you find that **i get hard** but lose the erection before or during intercourse, you are likely experiencing a common form of erectile dysfunction (ED). This specific issue—the inability to maintain firmness—is often caused by a "venous leak," where blood enters the penis but cannot be trapped effectively, or by psychological factors like performance anxiety. While achieving an initial erection is a positive sign of nerve and blood vessel function, maintaining it requires a complex balance of sustained blood flow, muscle relaxation, and mental focus. For more context, see our [complete guide erectile dysfunction](/blog/complete-guide-erectile-dysfunction).

## Why Maintenance is Different from Achievement

To understand why **i get hard but** cannot stay that way, it helps to look at the mechanics of an erection. When a man is aroused, the brain sends signals that relax the muscles in the penis, allowing blood to flow into two chambers called the corpora cavernosa. As these chambers fill, they compress the veins that normally carry blood away, effectively "trapping" the blood to maintain firmness.

If this trapping mechanism fails, it is often referred to as a venous leak. According to the [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/10035-erectile-dysfunction), maintaining an erection requires a constant "valvular" action; if the veins do not stay compressed, the erection will subside prematurely.

## Common Physical Causes

Several physical factors can interfere with your body's ability to hold blood in the penis:

- **Cardiovascular Health:** Conditions like high blood pressure or atherosclerosis can damage the lining of the blood vessels, making it harder for them to stay dilated or for the veins to stay compressed.
- **Venous Leak:** This occurs when the smooth muscle in the penis doesn't relax enough to compress the drainage veins. It is a frequent cause for men who find [my erection go away](https://acceleratehealth.com/blog/why-does-my-erection-go-away-before-penetration) shortly after it starts.
- **Hormonal Imbalances:** Low testosterone can reduce libido and the quality of the signals sent from the brain to the penis, leading to a "fading" erection.
- **Neurological Issues:** Conditions like diabetes can cause nerve damage, interrupting the signals required to keep the pelvic muscles relaxed.

For a deeper look at how these causes change over time, you can read our guide on [erectile dysfunction by age](https://acceleratehealth.com/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s).

## The Role of Psychological Factors

The brain is the most important sex organ. Even if your physical "plumbing" is working correctly, psychological interference can kill an erection instantly.

- **Performance Anxiety:** The fear of losing an erection often becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy. When you worry about your performance, your body releases adrenaline, which constricts blood vessels and reverses the erection process.
- **Spectatoring:** This is a psychological phenomenon where a man "steps outside" the moment to monitor his own erection. This lack of mental presence can cause the erection to fade.
- **Stress and Fatigue:** High levels of cortisol (the stress hormone) are known to interfere with the signals required for sustained arousal.

If you find that [i wake up morning](https://acceleratehealth.com/blog/why-do-i-wake-up-with-morning-wood-but-have-ed) with a firm erection but struggle during sex, the cause is more likely to be psychological or related to specific situational triggers rather than a permanent physical blockage.

## When to Speak with a Clinician

While occasional issues with maintaining an erection are normal, consistent difficulty should be discussed with a healthcare provider. This is especially important because ED can be an early warning sign of underlying cardiovascular disease.

**Important Safety Note:** If you are taking nitrates for chest pain (such as nitroglycerin) or use recreational "poppers," you must not take PDE5 inhibitors like sildenafil. This combination can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure. According to the [American Heart Association](https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/heart-attack/treatment-of-a-heart-attack/nitroglycerin-and-erectile-dysfunction-drugs), men with unstable cardiovascular disease should be evaluated by a doctor before using any ED medication.

Seek urgent medical care if you experience:
- An erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
- Sudden vision or hearing loss.
- Chest pain, dizziness, or symptoms of a heart attack during sexual activity.
