To read your ED lab results, focus on three main categories: hormonal health, metabolic function, and cardiovascular markers. Key values to look for include Total Testosterone (typically 300–1,000 ng/dL), Hemoglobin A1c (ideally below 5.7%), and Lipid profiles (LDL cholesterol). These markers help clinicians determine if erectile dysfunction is caused by low hormones, blood sugar issues, or poor circulation. While these numbers provide a baseline, they must be interpreted by a healthcare provider who considers your specific symptoms and medical history.

## Understanding Your Hormone Panel

Hormones play a critical role in sexual desire and erectile function. When you **read ed lab results**, the hormonal section is often the first place a clinician looks to rule out endocrine issues.

- **Total Testosterone:** This measures the total amount of testosterone in your blood. While the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/tests-procedures/testosterone-test/about/pac-20384885) notes a broad normal range of 300 to 1,000 ng/dL, many men experience symptoms of ED at the lower end of this spectrum.
- **Free Testosterone:** This measures the testosterone that is not bound to proteins and is "active" in your body. It can be a more accurate reflection of hormonal health if your Total Testosterone is borderline.
- **Prolactin:** High levels of this hormone can suppress testosterone production and decrease libido. According to the [Urology Care Foundation](https://www.urologyhealth.org/urology-a-z/e/erectile-dysfunction-(ed)), elevated prolactin may indicate a need for further neurological or endocrine evaluation.

Hormonal needs change as you get older. You can learn more about how these levels shift in our guide to [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s).

## Metabolic and Blood Sugar Markers

Erectile dysfunction is often an early warning sign of metabolic issues like diabetes. High blood sugar can damage the small blood vessels and nerves responsible for achieving an erection.

- **Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c):** This test measures your average blood sugar over the past three months. The [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/diabetes/managing/managing-blood-sugar/a1c.html) defines a normal A1c as below 5.7%. A result between 5.7% and 6.4% indicates prediabetes, which can significantly impact vascular health.
- **Fasting Glucose:** This measures your blood sugar at a single point in time after fasting. Consistent readings above 100 mg/dL may require further investigation.

If your labs suggest metabolic concerns, it is important to look at the [complete guide erectile dysfunction](/blog/complete-guide-erectile-dysfunction) to understand how lifestyle changes and medical treatments can work together.

## Cardiovascular and Lipid Profiles

Because erections are a vascular event, meaning they rely on healthy blood flow, your heart health markers are essential when you **read ed lab** reports.

- **LDL Cholesterol:** Often called "bad" cholesterol, high levels can lead to plaque buildup in the arteries (atherosclerosis), restricting blood flow to the penis.
- **HDL Cholesterol:** "Good" cholesterol helps remove other forms of cholesterol from your bloodstream.
- **Triglycerides:** High levels of these fats in the blood are often linked to a higher risk of heart disease and ED.

The [American Heart Association](https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/cholesterol/about-cholesterol/what-your-cholesterol-levels-mean) emphasizes that managing these lipids is vital for overall vascular health, which directly impacts erectile quality.

## Interpreting the Results with Your Doctor

Lab results are not a diagnosis on their own. They are pieces of a puzzle that your doctor uses to create a treatment plan. For example, if your labs are normal but you still experience ED, your clinician might explore psychological factors or suggest ways to [reduce anxiety before sex](/blog/how-to-reduce-anxiety-before-sex).

If your doctor determines that a PDE5 inhibitor is appropriate based on your labs, you will need to [pick right dose sildenafil](/blog/how-to-pick-the-right-dose-of-sildenafil) to ensure the medication is both safe and effective for your body chemistry.

## Important Safety Information

Before starting any treatment for ED, it is vital to understand that PDE5 inhibitors (like sildenafil) are contraindicated for men taking nitrates for chest pain or those using "poppers" (amyl nitrite). Combining these can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure.

Men with unstable cardiovascular disease, recent heart attack, or stroke should consult a specialist before using ED medications. Seek urgent medical care if you experience:
- Chest pain during sexual activity.
- An erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
- Sudden vision or hearing loss.
