The success rate of ED medication is generally high, with approximately **70% to 80% of men** experiencing improved erectile function when using phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) inhibitors like sildenafil or tadalafil. While these medications are the first-line treatment for erectile dysfunction, their effectiveness can vary based on the underlying cause of the condition, the user's overall health, and how the medication is administered.

## Understanding the Success Rate of ED Medication

Clinical data consistently shows that PDE5 inhibitors are effective for a broad range of men. According to the [Urology Care Foundation](https://www.urologyhealth.org/urology-a-z/e/erectile-dysfunction-(ed)), these oral medications work for about 7 out of 10 men. Success in these studies is typically defined as the ability to achieve and maintain an erection firm enough for sexual intercourse.

However, the "success rate" is not a static number. It can be influenced by several clinical factors:

- **The Cause of ED:** Medications tend to have higher success rates when ED is caused by psychological factors or mild vascular issues. If ED is the result of severe nerve damage (such as after certain prostate surgeries) or advanced diabetes, the success rate may be lower.
- **Proper Administration:** For many, "failure" of the medication is actually a result of improper use. For example, standard sildenafil tablets often require an empty stomach to work effectively, as high-fat meals can delay absorption.
- **Sexual Stimulation:** PDE5 inhibitors are not aphrodisiacs; they require physical or mental sexual arousal to trigger the chemical process that leads to an erection.

## Safety and Medical Considerations

It is essential to remember that while ED medications have a high success rate, they are not safe for everyone.

**Important Safety Warning:**
You must not take PDE5 inhibitors (like sildenafil or tadalafil) if you take nitrates for chest pain or have unstable cardiovascular disease. Combining these can cause a dangerous, life-threatening drop in blood pressure. Always consult a clinician before starting any ED treatment.

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:
- Chest pain, dizziness, or nausea during sex.
- An erection lasting more than four hours (priapism).
- Sudden loss of vision or hearing.

## Improving Your Results

To maximize the success rate of your ED medication, clinicians often recommend:
1. **Patience:** It may take several attempts (sometimes up to 8 doses) to see the full benefit of the medication.
2. **Timing:** Follow the specific instructions for your prescription regarding when to take the dose relative to sexual activity.
3. **Health Management:** Managing blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol can improve the health of the blood vessels that the medication targets.
