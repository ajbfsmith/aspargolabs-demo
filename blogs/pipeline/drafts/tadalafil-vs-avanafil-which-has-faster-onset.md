When comparing the two, **avanafil typically has a faster onset than tadalafil**. While tadalafil (Cialis) generally takes 30 to 60 minutes to reach its full effect, avanafil (Stendra) is often effective in as little as 15 to 30 minutes. This makes avanafil the fastest-acting oral tablet among the standard PDE5 inhibitors. However, tadalafil is known for its long duration, remaining effective for up to 36 hours, whereas avanafil’s effects usually last about six hours. Choosing the right option depends on whether you value a quick start or a long window of efficacy.

## Understanding Avanafil's Speed

Avanafil is a second-generation PDE5 inhibitor designed specifically to address the "wait time" associated with older medications. According to [clinical data from the FDA](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2012/202276s000lbl.pdf), avanafil reaches maximum plasma concentrations quickly, allowing some men to see results in approximately 15 minutes.

This rapid absorption is a primary differentiator in the [sildenafil tadalafil vardenafil avanafil](/blog/sildenafil-tadalafil-vardenafil-avanafil-comparison) comparison. Because it is highly selective for the PDE5 enzyme, it may also have a slightly lower risk of certain side effects, such as vision changes, compared to older drugs.

## Tadalafil: The "Weekend Pill"

While tadalafil avanafil has faster onset in the short term, tadalafil remains the leader in duration. Tadalafil is unique because it has a much longer half-life. While it may take closer to an hour to feel the full effects, those effects can persist for a day and a half.

This long-lasting nature is why tadalafil is often preferred by men who want spontaneity without having to time a pill precisely before sexual activity. ## Safety and Cardiovascular Considerations

Regardless of which medication has a faster onset, safety is the most important factor. All PDE5 inhibitors, including tadalafil and avanafil, work by dilating blood vessels. This can cause a significant drop in blood pressure if combined with other medications.

**Important Safety Warning:**
- **Nitrates:** You must never take tadalafil, avanafil, or sildenafil if you use organic nitrates (such as nitroglycerin) for chest pain or "poppers" (amyl nitrite). This combination can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure.
- **Cardiovascular Health:** Men with unstable cardiovascular disease, recent heart attack, or stroke must speak with a clinician before using any ED medication.
- **Urgent Care:** Seek immediate medical attention if you experience chest pain, sudden vision loss, sudden hearing loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
