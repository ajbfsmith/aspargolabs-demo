## The Reality of Post-Operative ED

Before my surgery, my doctor explained the risks. He used the term "nerve-sparing," which sounded promising. He told me that while most men experience some level of erectile dysfunction (ED) immediately after the procedure, many regain function over 12 to 24 months.

What I wasn't prepared for was the psychological toll of that waiting period. According to the [American Cancer Society](https://www.cancer.org/cancer/survivorship/coping/side-effects-in-men/sexual-health-issues-for-men.html), nearly all men experience some degree of ED immediately following a prostatectomy, even with the most advanced surgical techniques. The nerves responsible for erections are incredibly delicate and sit right next to the prostate; even the slightest trauma or inflammation can "put them to sleep" for a long time.

For me, the frustration wasn't just about the physical loss. It was about the loss of spontaneity. My wife and I have always been close, but the sudden wall between us in the bedroom started to affect our entire dynamic. I found myself avoiding physical touch altogether because I was afraid it would lead to a "moment" I couldn't finish. This is a common hurdle, and navigating [ed relationships erectile dysfunction](/blog/erectile-dysfunction-relationship-impact) is often the hardest part of the recovery process.

## A Note on Safety and Expectations

It is important to remember that ED after surgery is a complex medical issue. While innovations like HEZKUE can help manage the symptoms, they are not a "fix" for the underlying nerve healing that must occur. 

If you are experiencing **ed after my** surgery, you must speak with your clinician before starting any new treatment. This is especially critical if you:
- Take nitrates for chest pain (nitroglycerin).
- Have unstable cardiovascular disease.
- Experience sudden changes in vision or hearing.

PDE5 inhibitors can cause a dangerous drop in blood pressure when combined with nitrates or certain "poppers." Always seek urgent medical care if you experience chest pain or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
