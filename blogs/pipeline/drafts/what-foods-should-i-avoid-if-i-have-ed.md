Limiting alcohol is also recommended, as it acts as a central nervous system depressant that can hinder arousal and performance.

## Foods That Impact Blood Flow

Erectile function is largely a matter of blood flow. Anything that harms your heart or blood vessels will eventually affect your ability to achieve an erection.

### Saturated and Trans Fats
Diets high in saturated fats (found in red meats and full-fat dairy) and trans fats (found in many fried and packaged foods) can lead to atherosclerosis, or the hardening of the arteries. According to [Harvard Health Publishing](https://www.health.harvard.edu/mens-health/diet-and-erectile-dysfunction), the same dietary patterns that cause heart attacks by blocking coronary arteries can also block the smaller arteries that supply blood to the penis.

### High-Sodium Foods
Excessive salt intake is a leading cause of hypertension (high blood pressure). Over time, high blood pressure damages the lining of the blood vessels, making it harder for them to dilate when needed. Avoiding canned soups, processed meats, and fast food can help maintain the vascular elasticity required for healthy erections.

### Refined Sugars and Carbohydrates
A diet high in processed sugars can lead to obesity and Type 2 diabetes, two of the most significant risk factors for ED. High blood sugar levels damage the nerves and blood vessels throughout the body. Research published by the [American Diabetes Association](https://diabetes.org/health-wellness/sexual-health/erectile-dysfunction) notes that men with diabetes are three times more likely to experience ED than those without the condition.

## The Specific Risk of Grapefruit

If you are using medication to manage ED, your diet requires even more scrutiny. Grapefruit and grapefruit juice contain compounds that inhibit the CYP3A4 enzyme in the gut. This enzyme is responsible for breaking down many medications, including sildenafil.

When this enzyme is blocked, the concentration of the medication in your bloodstream can rise to unsafe levels, increasing the risk of side effects like headaches, flushing, or dangerously low blood pressure. If you are curious about the [best time take sildenafil?](/blog/what-is-the-best-time-to-take-sildenafil), remember that timing it around a grapefruit-heavy breakfast is a significant safety risk.

## Alcohol and ED

While a drink might seem like it helps with "performance anxiety," alcohol is a depressant that slows down the central nervous system. It can interfere with the signals between the brain and the body that trigger an erection. Chronic heavy drinking is also linked to reduced testosterone production and permanent nerve damage.

## Important Safety Information

Before making significant changes to your diet or starting a new medication, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider.

- **Nitrates:** Never take PDE5 inhibitors (like sildenafil) if you are also taking nitrates for chest pain or pulmonary hypertension. This combination can cause a fatal drop in blood pressure.
- **Cardiovascular Health:** If you have unstable heart disease or have recently suffered a heart attack or stroke, speak with a doctor before engaging in sexual activity or starting ED treatment.
- **Emergency Symptoms:** Seek immediate medical attention if you experience chest pain during sex, sudden vision or hearing loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
