If your erection keeps going soft during intimacy, you are likely experiencing "maintenance erectile dysfunction." This occurs when blood flow to the penis is insufficient to sustain an erection or when the nervous system signals the body to exit the "arousal" state prematurely. Common causes range from performance anxiety and stress to physical issues like poor circulation or venous leak. While frustrating, it is a treatable condition often addressed through lifestyle changes, therapy, or clinically formulated medications.

## Understanding Maintenance Erectile Dysfunction

Erectile dysfunction (ED) is often misunderstood as a total inability to achieve an erection. However, many men find that they can achieve an erection initially, but **my erection keeps going** soft before or during intercourse. This specific challenge is known as maintenance ED.

According to the [Urology Care Foundation](https://www.urologyhealth.org/urology-a-z/e/erectile-dysfunction-(ed)), an erection requires a complex coordination of the brain, nerves, hormones, and blood vessels. If any part of this chain is interrupted, the "trapdoor" mechanism that keeps blood inside the penis may fail, causing the erection to subside prematurely.

## Why My Erection Keeps Losing Firmness: Common Causes

There is rarely a single reason why an erection fades. Usually, it is a combination of physical and psychological factors.

### 1. Performance Anxiety and Stress
The most common reason an erection disappears mid-activity is the "adrenaline spike" caused by performance anxiety. When you worry about whether you can stay hard, your body enters a "fight or flight" mode. This releases norepinephrine, a chemical that constricts blood vessels and effectively kills an erection. You can learn more about how this cycle works in our guide to [psychological erectile dysfunction anxiety](/blog/psychological-erectile-dysfunction-guide).

### 2. Physical and Vascular Issues
For an erection to stay firm, blood must flow in and stay trapped. If the blood vessels are damaged—often due to high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or diabetes—the flow may not be strong enough to maintain pressure. In some cases, a "venous leak" occurs, where the veins cannot properly compress to keep blood in the penis. This is a core topic covered in our [complete guide erectile dysfunction](/blog/complete-guide-erectile-dysfunction).

### 3. Lifestyle and Habits
Alcohol is a central nervous system depressant that can make it difficult to maintain an erection, even if you had no trouble getting one initially. Similarly, nicotine constricts blood vessels, making it harder for the body to keep blood localized where it’s needed. If you notice that [i had great erection](/blog/i-had-a-great-erection-last-night-but-not-today-why) recently but are struggling now, lifestyle factors or temporary fatigue might be the culprit.

## Does Age Play a Role?
While ED becomes more common as men get older, the reasons vary significantly by decade. Younger men are more likely to experience maintenance issues due to anxiety or substance use, while older men may face more vascular or hormonal challenges. You can explore these differences in our breakdown of [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s).

## When to See a Doctor
If **my erection keeps** going soft regularly, it is important to consult a healthcare provider to rule out underlying cardiovascular issues. ED can sometimes be an early warning sign of heart disease or circulatory problems.

**Important Safety Note:**
- PDE5 inhibitors (like sildenafil) are strictly contraindicated for men taking nitrates (often prescribed for chest pain) or riociguat.
- Men with unstable cardiovascular disease or serious heart symptoms must speak with a clinician before seeking ED treatment.
- Seek urgent medical care if you experience chest pain, symptoms of a heart attack, sudden vision loss, sudden hearing loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
