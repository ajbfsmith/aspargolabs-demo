## The Connection Between Sleep and Testosterone

For men, sleep isn't just about rest; it's a diagnostic window for hormonal health. Most testosterone production occurs during deep, restorative sleep, particularly during REM cycles. When you work nights, your body struggles to reach these deep stages during the day because of light exposure and noise.

Research has shown that even one week of sleep restriction can drop testosterone levels by 10% to 15% in healthy young men. According to the [Journal of the American Medical Association (JAMA)](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/1029127), sleep loss significantly lowers testosterone, which can lead to reduced energy, low libido, and poor concentration.
