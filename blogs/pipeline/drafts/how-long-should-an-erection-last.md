For most men, a healthy erection should last from the beginning of sexual stimulation until climax or the end of sexual activity. There is no specific "correct" number of minutes, as duration varies based on age, health, and arousal levels. However, from a medical perspective, any erection that lasts for more than four hours—especially if it occurs without sexual stimulation or is painful—is considered a medical emergency known as priapism.

## What is a "Normal" Erection Duration?

The question of how **long should erection last?** is common, but the answer is highly individual. In a clinical sense, an erection is considered functional if it is firm enough for penetration and lasts long enough for both partners to feel satisfied. 

According to the [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/10035-erectile-dysfunction), the physiological process of an erection involves blood flowing into the chambers of the penis and being "trapped" there by the surrounding tissues. Once stimulation ends or ejaculation occurs, the blood should flow back out, and the penis returns to a flaccid state.

## When an Erection Lasts Too Long: Priapism

While many men worry about an erection ending too soon, an erection that lasts too long is a serious medical concern. Priapism is a condition where blood becomes trapped in the penis for an extended period.

- **Ischemic Priapism:** This is the most common and dangerous form. It occurs when blood cannot leave the penis. It is often painful and can lead to permanent tissue damage or permanent erectile dysfunction if not treated within hours.
- **The Four-Hour Rule:** If an erection persists for more than four hours, you must seek immediate emergency medical care.

According to the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/priapism/symptoms-causes/syc-20352005), prompt treatment is necessary to prevent scarring and long-term loss of function.

## Factors That Affect How Long an Erection Lasts

Several factors influence the quality and duration of an erection. Understanding these can help you determine if your experience is within a healthy range or if you should consult a professional.

### 1. Age and Physical Health
As men age, the blood vessels and nerves that facilitate erections may become less efficient. You can read more about [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s) to see what is typical for your stage of life. Conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and heart disease can also significantly impact how **long should erection** stay firm.

### 2. Psychological Factors
Stress, performance anxiety, and depression can all interfere with the brain's ability to maintain the signals required for a sustained erection. If you are unsure if your concerns are rooted in your body or your mind, exploring [how do i know if my ed is physical or mental](/blog/how-do-i-know-if-my-ed-is-physical-or-mental) can provide clarity.

### 3. Medications and Substances
Certain medications, including some antidepressants and blood pressure drugs, can affect erectile duration. Conversely, PDE5 inhibitors (the class of drugs used to treat ED) are designed to help maintain an erection during stimulation, but they should still subside after the activity is over.

## When to Speak With a Clinician

If you are concerned about the duration of your erections—whether they are too short or you are experiencing prolonged, painful erections—it is important to seek medical advice. A clinician can help determine if there is an underlying cardiovascular issue or if a change in medication is necessary.

You must not take these medications if you use nitrates for chest pain or have unstable cardiovascular disease.
