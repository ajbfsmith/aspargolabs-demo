## The Interview: A Sleep Specialist on the Link Between Sleep and ED

**Accelerate Health: Why is a sleep specialist so often involved in the conversation about erectile dysfunction?**

**Expert:** It comes down to biology. Most people don't realize that the body’s "maintenance work" for sexual function happens while you sleep. Testosterone production, vascular repair, and even the neurological pathways required for an erection are all heavily dependent on high-quality, uninterrupted sleep. When I see a patient for snoring or insomnia, I’m often looking at the same vascular and hormonal markers that a urologist looks at for ED.

**Accelerate Health: What is the most common sleep disorder you see linked to sexual health issues?**

**Expert:** Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA) is the primary culprit. Research published in the [Journal of Sexual Medicine](https://academic.oup.com/jsm/article-abstract/6/11/3105/6829778) has shown that men with OSA are significantly more likely to experience ED. In fact, some studies suggest that nearly 70% of men with OSA also suffer from some degree of erectile dysfunction. 

OSA causes repeated drops in blood oxygen levels during the night. This puts immense stress on the vascular system and reduces the production of nitric oxide, which is the chemical messenger required to relax the blood vessels in the penis to allow for blood flow.

**Accelerate Health: How does general sleep deprivation—just not getting enough hours—affect performance?**

**Expert:** It’s a double-edged sword of hormones and stress. First, testosterone levels are highest during REM sleep. If you are cutting your sleep short, you are literally cutting off your testosterone supply. A study in [JAMA](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/1029127) found that just one week of sleep restriction (five hours per night) can lower testosterone levels by 10% to 15% in healthy young men.

Second, sleep deprivation spikes cortisol, the stress hormone. High cortisol is an "anti-erection" hormone; it keeps the body in a "fight or flight" state, making it very difficult to transition into the relaxed state necessary for arousal. This is why understanding [erectile dysfunction by age](https://acceleratehealth.com/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s) is so important—younger men are often more prone to sleep-deprivation-related ED due to lifestyle factors.

**Accelerate Health: We often hear about "morning wood." Does a lack of these nocturnal erections signal a problem?**

**Expert:** Yes. These are technically called Nocturnal Penile Tumescence (NPT). A healthy man typically has three to five erections per night during REM sleep. These aren't necessarily related to sexual dreams; they are the body’s way of oxygenating the penile tissue to keep it healthy and elastic. If you stop having these, it’s often a sign that your sleep cycles are fragmented or that there is an underlying vascular issue. This is a core concept we discuss in our [complete guide erectile dysfunction](https://acceleratehealth.com/blog/complete-guide-erectile-dysfunction).
