If you find yourself thinking, "i got side effect symptoms from my ED medication," the first step is to determine if the reaction is a common nuisance or a medical emergency. Most side effects from PDE5 inhibitors like sildenafil are mild and temporary, such as flushing or a headache. However, you must call a doctor or seek emergency care immediately if you experience chest pain, sudden vision or hearing loss, or an erection lasting more than four hours (priapism). ## Common Side Effects: What to Expect
Most men who use PDE5 inhibitors will experience mild side effects at some point. These occur because the medication relaxes smooth muscle tissue not just in the penis, but throughout the body.

Common, non-emergency side effects include:
- **Headaches:** The most frequently reported side effect due to changes in blood flow.
- **Flushing:** A warm, red feeling on the face, neck, or chest.
- **Indigestion:** Often described as an upset stomach or heartburn.
- **Nasal Congestion:** A "stuffy" nose caused by the dilation of blood vessels in the nasal passages.
- **Back or Muscle Pain:** More common with certain long-acting ED medications.

If you find yourself saying "i got side effect" symptoms like these, they typically fade as the medication leaves your system. If they persist or become bothersome, consult your doctor about adjusting your dosage.

## When to Seek Emergency Medical Care
While rare, some side effects require immediate intervention to prevent permanent damage. If you experience any of the following, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room:

### 1. Priapism (Prolonged Erection)
An erection that lasts longer than four hours is a medical emergency known as priapism. According to the [Urology Care Foundation](https://www.urologyhealth.org/urologic-conditions/priapism), untreated priapism can lead to permanent tissue damage and irreversible erectile dysfunction.

### 2. Sudden Vision or Hearing Loss
In rare cases, users have reported a sudden decrease or loss of vision in one or both eyes, which may be a sign of Non-Arteritic Ischemic Optic Neuropathy (NAION). Similarly, sudden hearing loss or ringing in the ears (tinnitus) requires immediate medical evaluation, as noted by the [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-availability-information-patients-and-providers/pde5-inhibitors-erectile-dysfunction-and-sudden-hearing-loss).

### 3. Chest Pain and Heart Symptoms
If you experience chest pain, dizziness, or nausea during sexual activity after taking ED medication, stop immediately. These can be signs of a serious cardiovascular event.

## The Danger of Nitrates and Contraindications
You should never take sildenafil or other PDE5 inhibitors if you are taking nitrates for chest pain (angina) or pulmonary hypertension. This combination can cause a sudden, life-threatening drop in blood pressure. This includes prescription medications like nitroglycerin and recreational "poppers" (amyl nitrate).

Men with unstable cardiovascular disease or those who have been advised by a doctor to avoid sexual activity due to heart health should not use these medications. If you find that "i took sildenafil didn't" work as expected or caused mild discomfort, there are ways to manage the experience:
- **Hydration:** Drinking water can sometimes help mitigate headaches.
- **Timing:** Taking the medication with or without food (depending on the specific drug) can change how your body processes it.
