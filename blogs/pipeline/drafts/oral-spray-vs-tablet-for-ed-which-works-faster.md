## Why tablets take longer to work

The traditional oral tablet is the most common delivery method for ED medications like [sildenafil tadalafil vardenafil avanafil](/blog/sildenafil-tadalafil-vardenafil-avanafil-comparison). However, the journey from swallowing a pill to achieving an effect is relatively long.

When you take a tablet, your body must first break the solid mass down into smaller particles (disintegration). Once disintegrated, those particles must dissolve into the gastric fluids (dissolution) before the medication can be absorbed through the intestinal wall and enter the bloodstream. According to the [FDA-approved labeling for sildenafil tablets](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2014/020895s039s040lbl.pdf), the average time to reach peak plasma concentration is approximately 60 minutes, though some men may feel effects sooner.

## The impact of food on ED medication

One of the most significant drawbacks of traditional tablets is the "food effect." Taking a sildenafil tablet with a high-fat meal can delay the onset of action by up to 60 minutes and reduce the peak concentration of the drug in the blood by nearly 30%. This is because the digestive system is occupied with processing fats, which slows the absorption of the solid pill.

Oral spray suspensions may mitigate some of this delay. While the medication still ends up in the digestive tract, the pre-solubilized nature of a spray allows it to be processed more efficiently than a solid tablet that must compete with food for dissolution.

You must speak with a clinician if you have:
-   Unstable cardiovascular disease or a history of heart attack or stroke.
-   Severe liver or kidney impairment.
-   Serious symptoms like chest pain during sexual activity.

Seek urgent medical care immediately if you experience an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism), sudden vision loss in one or both eyes, or sudden hearing loss. These medications are not a "miracle cure" and are not safe for everyone.
