## The Prevalence of Erectile Dysfunction

To understand medication usage, we must first look at the scale of the condition. According to the [National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases (NIDDK)](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/erectile-dysfunction/definition-facts), erectile dysfunction affects approximately 30 million men in the United States alone. 

The likelihood of experiencing ED increases with age, but it is not exclusive to older populations. Research often cites the Massachusetts Male Aging Study, which found that about 40% of men experience some degree of ED by age 40, a figure that increases to 70% by age 70. ## How Many Men Take ED Medication?

While millions of men experience symptoms, the number of men who regularly use medication has historically been lower due to barriers like cost, embarrassment, and lack of access. However, recent trends show a significant uptick in prescriptions.

1. **Prescription Trends:** Data published in [Scientific Reports](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-91754-w) indicates a steady global increase in the use of PDE5 inhibitors (the class of drugs that includes sildenafil and tadalafil) over the last decade.
2. **Survey Data:** Independent health surveys suggest that nearly 20% of men over the age of 40 have used ED medication at least once, with a growing percentage using it on a regular "as-needed" basis.
3. **Younger Demographics:** There is a notable rise in usage among men in their 20s and 30s. This is often attributed to increased awareness, the impact of performance anxiety, and the ease of obtaining prescriptions through digital health platforms.

The shift toward regular usage is also linked to the [telehealth ED usage trends](/blog/telehealth-for-ed-usage-trends-and-patient-satisfaction-data) we see today. ## Important Safety Information

While ED medications are effective for many, they are medical treatments that require clinical oversight. 

**Contraindications and Warnings:**
- **Nitrates:** PDE5 inhibitors (including sildenafil) are strictly contraindicated for men taking nitrates for chest pain or heart conditions. Taking these together can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure.
- **Cardiovascular Health:** Men with unstable cardiovascular disease or recent heart attack/stroke must consult a clinician before use.
- **Seek Urgent Care:** If you experience chest pain, sudden vision loss, sudden hearing loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism), seek emergency medical attention immediately.

ED medication is not a "one-size-fits-all" solution. Always speak with a healthcare provider to ensure that any treatment is safe for your specific health profile.
