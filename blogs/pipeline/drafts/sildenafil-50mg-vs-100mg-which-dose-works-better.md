The 100mg dose is the maximum recommended amount, often prescribed if 50mg is insufficient. ## The Standard Starting Point: Sildenafil 50mg

The 50mg dose is the most common starting point for men seeking treatment for erectile dysfunction (ED). According to [FDA prescribing guidelines](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2014/020895s044lbl.pdf), this middle-ground dose is designed to provide significant improvement in erectile function for the majority of men while minimizing the risk of adverse reactions.

At 50mg, the medication typically takes 30 to 60 minutes to become effective. For many, this dose provides a reliable window of activity that lasts about four hours. It is often the preferred choice for those exploring [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s), as younger men or those with mild-to-moderate ED often find 50mg more than sufficient.

## When to Consider the 100mg Dose

If the 50mg dose does not produce a satisfactory result after several attempts, a clinician may increase the prescription to 100mg. This is the highest dose of sildenafil approved for the treatment of ED.

Clinical trials published by the [National Institutes of Health (NIH)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11913473/) indicate that while 100mg is more effective than 50mg, the jump in efficacy is incremental rather than a doubling of power. The 100mg dose is generally reserved for men with more severe ED or those with underlying health conditions, such as diabetes or post-prostatectomy recovery, which may make the nerves and blood vessels less responsive to lower doses of PDE5 inhibitors.

## Side Effects: The Trade-off of Higher Doses

The primary drawback of the 100mg dose is the increased frequency and intensity of side effects. Because sildenafil works by dilating blood vessels, its effects are not limited strictly to the penis.

Common dose-dependent side effects include:

- **Headaches:** Reported by approximately 16% of men on 50mg, rising to 28% on 100mg.
- **Flushing:** A feeling of warmth or redness in the face and neck.
- **Dyspepsia:** Indigestion or upset stomach.
- **Visual Changes:** Such as a blue tint to vision or increased sensitivity to light.

While most side effects are mild and temporary, the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/drugs-supplements/sildenafil-oral-route/side-effects/drg-20066989) notes that the risk of these issues increases significantly as the dosage moves from 50mg to 100mg.

## Important Safety Considerations

Regardless of whether you are prescribed 50mg or 100mg, sildenafil is a potent medication that affects the cardiovascular system. It is part of a class of drugs called PDE5 inhibitors, which you can learn more about in our [sildenafil tadalafil vardenafil avanafil](/blog/sildenafil-tadalafil-vardenafil-avanafil-comparison) comparison.

**You must not take sildenafil if you:**
- Take nitrates (such as nitroglycerin) for chest pain, as this can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure.
- Use recreational "poppers" (amyl nitrate).
- Have unstable cardiovascular disease or have been advised by a doctor to avoid sexual activity.

Seek urgent medical care if you experience:
- Chest pain or symptoms of a heart attack.
- An erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism), which can cause permanent damage.
- Sudden loss of vision in one or both eyes.
- Sudden decrease or loss of hearing.
