To **get an ED prescription online**, you must select a licensed telehealth platform, complete a digital medical assessment, and undergo a review by a licensed healthcare provider. If the clinician determines that treatment is safe and appropriate for you, they will issue a prescription for a phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) inhibitor, such as sildenafil. This prescription can then be fulfilled by a mail-order pharmacy or sent to your local druggist.

Obtaining a prescription online has become a standard way to address erectile dysfunction (ED) with privacy and clinical oversight. However, it is not as simple as "buying" medication; it is a medical process designed to ensure that ED treatments do not conflict with your existing health conditions or medications.

## Step 1: Select a Licensed Telehealth Platform

The first step is choosing a reputable provider. Look for platforms that use U.S.-licensed physicians and pharmacists. Reputable sites will often display certifications from organizations like [LegitScript](https://www.legitscript.com/), which verifies that the platform operates legally and safely. 

Avoid websites that offer to sell "generic Viagra" or sildenafil without a prescription. According to the [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/consumers/consumer-updates/how-buy-medicines-safely-online-pharmacy), medications sold without a valid prescription are often counterfeit and may contain dangerous ingredients or incorrect dosages.

## Step 2: Complete a Medical Health Assessment

Once you choose a platform, you will fill out a detailed medical intake form. This is a critical part of the process where you will share:

- Your current symptoms and how long you have experienced ED.
- Your medical history, specifically focusing on heart health and blood pressure.
- A list of all current medications and supplements.
- Your recent blood pressure readings.

Because ED can be a precursor to other health issues, understanding your [complete guide erectile dysfunction](/blog/complete-guide-erectile-dysfunction) is essential for a clinician to provide a safe recommendation.

## Step 3: Clinician Review and Consultation

A licensed healthcare provider will review your assessment. Depending on the state you live in, this may happen through an "asynchronous" review (where the doctor reviews your data and messages you) or a "synchronous" visit (a live video or phone call).

The clinician’s job is to determine if a PDE5 inhibitor is appropriate for you. They will screen for contraindications, such as the use of nitrates for chest pain, which can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure when combined with ED medication. They may also discuss [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s) to determine if further testing for underlying conditions like diabetes or low testosterone is necessary.

## Step 4: Prescription Fulfillment and Delivery

If approved, the clinician will write a prescription. Most online platforms have an integrated pharmacy that ships the medication in discreet packaging to your home. Alternatively, you can often request that the prescription be sent to a local pharmacy of your choice.

## Important Safety Considerations

While getting an ED prescription online is convenient, it is not a substitute for regular physical exams. You should speak with a clinician in person if you have unstable cardiovascular disease, severe heart failure, or if you are taking nitrates or alpha-blockers.

According to the [American Urological Association](https://www.auanet.org/guidelines-and-quality/guidelines/erectile-dysfunction-guidelines), ED is often linked to cardiovascular health. If you experience chest pain, symptoms of a heart attack, or a prolonged erection lasting more than four hours (priapism), seek urgent medical care immediately.
