To use a vacuum erection device (VED), you place your penis into a plastic cylinder, create an airtight seal against your body with lubricant, and use a manual or battery-powered pump to draw air out. This vacuum pulls blood into the penis to create an erection. Once erect, you slide a constriction ring from the base of the cylinder onto the base of the penis to maintain the erection, then remove the cylinder.

A vacuum erection device is a non-invasive treatment option often recommended for men who cannot take oral medications or who are recovering from prostate surgery. While it requires some practice to master, it is a highly effective tool for managing erectile dysfunction (ED).

## Step-by-Step Guide to Using a VED

Using a VED correctly ensures both effectiveness and safety. Following these steps can help you achieve the best results:

1.  **Prepare the Device:** Ensure the constriction ring (tension ring) is placed on the end of the cylinder that will be against your body.
2.  **Apply Lubricant:** Use a water-soluble lubricant on the head and shaft of the penis, as well as the rim of the cylinder. This is essential for creating an airtight seal and preventing skin irritation.
3.  **Create a Seal:** Place your penis into the cylinder and press the base firmly against your pelvic bone. A tight seal is necessary for the vacuum to work.
4.  **Operate the Pump:** Slowly pump the air out of the cylinder. If using a manual pump, use steady strokes. If battery-powered, activate the switch. You will see the penis begin to engorge with blood.
5.  **Maintain the Erection:** Once the penis is sufficiently erect, slide the constriction ring off the cylinder and onto the base of your penis. This keeps the blood from flowing back into the body.
6.  **Release the Vacuum:** Use the release valve to let air back into the cylinder, then remove the device.

According to the [Urology Care Foundation](https://www.urologyhealth.org/urology-a-z/e/erectile-dysfunction(ed)), the entire process usually takes about five to ten minutes.

## Safety Precautions and Best Practices

While VEDs are generally safe, they must be used with caution to avoid tissue damage or bruising.

### The 30-Minute Rule
The most critical safety rule is to never leave the constriction ring on for more than 30 minutes. Leaving the ring on too long can restrict oxygen-rich blood flow, potentially leading to tissue damage. If you notice the penis becoming cold, numb, or significantly discolored, remove the ring immediately.

### Potential Side Effects
It is common for the penis to feel slightly cooler or have a bluish tint while the ring is in place. Some men also experience "hinging," where the base of the penis is not as rigid as the shaft. If you experience pain or significant bruising, consult a healthcare provider.

### Who Should Avoid VEDs?
Men with bleeding disorders or those taking blood-thinning medications (like warfarin or heparin) should consult a doctor before using a VED, as the vacuum can cause bruising or "petechiae" (small red spots) on the skin. ## Comparing VEDs to Other Treatments

A vacuum device is often used when oral medications are contraindicated or ineffective. However, many men find the process of using a VED cumbersome compared to the simplicity of a pill or spray.

If you are [taking ed medication first](/blog/first-time-ed-medication-what-to-expect), you may find it offers a more spontaneous experience. However, for those who cannot take PDE5 inhibitors due to health reasons, the VED remains a reliable mechanical alternative. Some men also combine VED use with [pelvic floor exercises ed](/blog/how-to-do-pelvic-floor-exercises-for-ed) to improve natural blood flow over time.

## Important Medical Warning

Erectile dysfunction can be an early warning sign of cardiovascular disease. If you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, or symptoms of a heart attack or stroke, seek emergency medical care immediately.

**Note on Nitrates:** If you take nitrates (such as nitroglycerin) for chest pain or heart problems, or if you use "poppers" (amyl nitrate), you must not use PDE5 inhibitor medications. Combining these can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure. Always speak with a clinician before starting a new ED treatment, especially if you have unstable cardiovascular disease.
