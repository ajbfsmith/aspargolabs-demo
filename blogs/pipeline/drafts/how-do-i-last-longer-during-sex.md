## Behavioral Techniques for Better Control

Many men find that they can increase their stamina by practicing specific behavioral techniques that help them recognize and delay the "point of no return."

- **The Stop-Start Method:** This involves stimulating the penis until you feel close to orgasm, then stopping all stimulation for about 30 seconds until the sensation passes. According to the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/premature-ejaculation/diagnosis-treatment/drc-20354905), repeating this process three times before allowing ejaculation can help train the body to delay climax.
- **The Squeeze Technique:** Similar to stop-start, this method requires you or your partner to firmly squeeze the end of the penis (where the head meets the shaft) for several seconds when you feel an orgasm approaching. This reduces the urge to ejaculate and may help you stay in the moment longer.

## Physical Conditioning and Pelvic Floor Health

The muscles that support the pelvic organs also play a role in sexual function. Strengthening the pubococcygeus (PC) muscles through Kegel exercises can improve your ability to delay ejaculation and maintain an erection.

Research published by [Harvard Health](https://www.health.harvard.edu/mens-health/step-by-step-guide-to-kegel-exercises-for-men) suggests that pelvic floor exercises can help men manage both premature ejaculation and ED. To perform a Kegel, contract the muscles you would use to stop the flow of urine, hold for three seconds, and release for three seconds. Repeating this cycle 10 times, three times a day, can lead to noticeable improvements in control over time.

Sometimes, the feeling that "I can't last" isn't about climaxing too early, but rather about losing an erection before the encounter is over. This is a common symptom of erectile dysfunction. ## Lifestyle and Psychological Factors

Sexual stamina is closely linked to overall health. Factors that improve blood flow and reduce stress often help ensure **i last longer** during sex.

1. **Cardiovascular Exercise:** Since erections are a vascular event, a healthy heart supports better performance.
2. **Managing Anxiety:** Performance anxiety can trigger the "fight or flight" response, which often leads to premature climax or loss of erection.
3. **Age-Related Changes:** It is normal for sexual response to change over time. Understanding [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s) can help set realistic expectations and identify when medical intervention might be necessary.

## Safety and Clinical Considerations

Before starting any new treatment or exercise regimen for sexual health, it is vital to speak with a healthcare provider. 

**Important Safety Information:**
- PDE5 inhibitors (like sildenafil) are strictly contraindicated for men taking nitrates for chest pain or heart conditions, as the combination can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure.
- Men with unstable cardiovascular disease or serious heart symptoms must be cleared by a clinician before engaging in sexual activity or using ED medications.
- Seek urgent medical care if you experience chest pain, symptoms of a heart attack, or a "priapism" (an erection lasting longer than four hours).
