Losing an erection while putting on or wearing a condom is a common experience known medically as Condom-Associated Erection Problems (CAEP). If you find that **i lose my erection** during this specific moment, it is often due to a combination of reduced physical sensation, the psychological "interruption" of the sexual flow, or performance anxiety. While it can be frustrating, CAEP is frequently manageable through better fit, practice, or addressing underlying blood flow issues.

## Understanding Condom-Associated Erection Problems (CAEP)

Research suggests that CAEP is more common than many men realize. A study published in the [Journal of Sexual Medicine](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25963453/) found that a significant percentage of men report losing their erection either while applying a condom or during intercourse with one. 

This isn't necessarily a sign of permanent erectile dysfunction (ED). Instead, it is often a situational response. However, if you find that **i lose my** erection even without a condom, it may be helpful to consult a [complete guide erectile dysfunction](/blog/complete-guide-erectile-dysfunction) to understand broader physiological causes.

## Why It Happens: The Psychological "Pause"

For many, the act of stopping to reach for and apply a condom creates a mental "break" in arousal. This interruption can trigger performance anxiety.

- **The Spectator Effect:** Once you worry about losing your erection, your brain shifts from a state of arousal to a state of monitoring. This anxiety triggers the sympathetic nervous system (the "fight or flight" response), which constricts blood vessels and makes maintaining an erection difficult.
- **Distraction:** The tactile focus required to put a condom on correctly can pull your mind away from the sexual stimulation needed to stay hard.

If this happens frequently, you might find yourself asking, "[can't i stay hard](/blog/why-can-t-i-stay-hard-during-sex)?" even when the condom isn't the primary issue.

## Physical Factors and Sensation

Beyond the psychological aspect, there are physical reasons why a condom might interfere with your erection:

- **Reduced Sensitivity:** Condoms create a physical barrier that reduces the direct friction and heat felt by the penis. For some men, this slight reduction in sensation is enough to cause the erection to subside.
- **Poor Fit:** A condom that is too tight can be uncomfortable and restrictive, while one that is too loose may slip, causing the user to focus more on the condom than the experience.
- **Lack of Lubrication:** If the condom causes friction or dryness, it can lead to discomfort, which quickly kills arousal.

## How to Maintain an Erection with a Condom

If you struggle with CAEP, there are several practical steps you can take to improve the experience:

1. **Practice Alone:** Desensitize the "interruption" by practicing putting on a condom during masturbation. This helps your brain associate the condom with pleasure rather than a stressful pause.
2. **Use Lubricant:** Adding a drop of water-based lubricant inside the tip of the condom can increase sensitivity and make the application smoother.
3. **Find the Right Fit:** Not all condoms are the same size. Experimenting with different brands and "ultra-thin" varieties can help restore lost sensation.
4. **Incorporate It into Foreplay:** Have your partner help put the condom on to keep the momentum and physical contact going.

If the issue persists despite these changes, it may be related to [my erection go away](/blog/why-does-my-erection-go-away-before-penetration) due to early-stage ED or age-related changes. You can learn more about how these factors change over time in our guide on [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s).

## When to Seek Medical Advice

While losing an erection with a condom is often situational, it can sometimes be an early indicator of underlying vascular or neurological issues. If you experience persistent difficulty, a clinician can help determine if a PDE5 inhibitor or other treatment is appropriate.

**Important Safety Note:** If you are considering medical treatments for ED, you must speak with a healthcare provider first. PDE5 inhibitors (like sildenafil) are strictly contraindicated for men taking nitrates (often prescribed for chest pain) or those with unstable cardiovascular disease. Combining these can lead to a dangerous drop in blood pressure. Always seek urgent medical care if you experience chest pain, sudden vision loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
