## Suspension vs. Solution: The Technical Distinction

In pharmaceutical science, liquids are generally categorized as either solutions or suspensions.

A **solution** is a homogeneous mixture where the active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) is completely dissolved in a solvent. Think of it like sugar fully dissolved in water; the molecules are evenly distributed and will not settle over time.

A **suspension**, such as HEZKUE, is a heterogeneous mixture. In this format, finely divided solid particles of the medication (like sildenafil) are dispersed throughout a liquid vehicle but are not fully dissolved. This distinction is critical for several reasons:

- **Drug Concentration:** Suspensions often allow for a higher concentration of the active compound than a solution might permit, especially if the drug has low solubility in water.
- **Chemical Stability:** Some active ingredients are more stable in a solid, suspended state than when fully dissolved, which can prevent the medication from degrading too quickly.
- **Taste Masking:** Because the drug particles are not fully dissolved, they can often be formulated to bypass the bitter taste receptors more effectively than a solution.

According to research published in [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/pharmacology-toxicology-and-pharmaceutical-science/pharmaceutical-suspension), the primary challenge in creating a suspension is ensuring that the particles remain evenly dispersed so that every dose is consistent.

## Particle Size Distribution and Micronization

The effectiveness of a suspension depends heavily on **particle size distribution**. If the particles of sildenafil are too large, they will settle at the bottom of the container (sedimentation), leading to inconsistent dosing.

To solve this, pharmaceutical engineers use a process called micronization. By reducing the active ingredient to a microscopic powder, the surface area of the drug is vastly increased. This serves two purposes:

1. **Uniformity:** Smaller particles stay suspended in the liquid for longer periods, ensuring that each spray contains the exact intended dose.
2. **Dissolution Rate:** Although the drug is in a liquid carrier, the body still needs to "dissolve" the solid particles. A higher surface area allows for faster dissolution once the spray contacts the oral mucosa (the lining of the mouth).

## Spray Droplet Size Optimization (10–100 Microns)

What **makes oral spray** delivery unique is not just the liquid itself, but the mechanics of the spray plume. When you press the actuator, the liquid is forced through a nozzle designed to create specific droplet sizes.

For oral delivery, the target droplet size typically falls within the **10 to 100 micron range**. This range is chosen for specific physiological reasons:

- **Avoiding Inhalation:** If droplets are smaller than 10 microns, there is a risk they could be inhaled into the lungs (pulmonary delivery) rather than staying in the mouth.
- **Maximizing Surface Area:** Droplets larger than 100 microns may be too heavy, leading them to pool in the mouth and be swallowed immediately, which reverts the delivery method back to traditional GI tract absorption.
- **Mucosal Adhesion:** Droplets in the 10–100 micron range are optimized to coat the tissues of the mouth, where the medication can begin to interact with the highly vascularized oral lining.

Research on [oromucosal drug delivery](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4401168/) suggests that bypassing the "first-pass metabolism" of the liver—which happens when you swallow a pill—can lead to more consistent plasma levels of the medication.

## Stability and Preservation of the Active Compound

Maintaining the integrity of sildenafil in a liquid state is more complex than keeping it in a dry tablet. Tablets are protected by binders and coatings that shield the API from oxygen and moisture. In a spray suspension, the formulation must include:

- **Suspending Agents:** These increase the viscosity of the liquid to prevent particles from settling.
- **Surfactants:** These help the liquid spread evenly across the oral mucosa.
- **Preservatives:** Because the formulation contains water or other liquids, it must be protected against microbial growth to ensure a long shelf life.

Stability data requirements for oral sprays are rigorous. Manufacturers must prove that the drug remains potent and the suspension remains uniform across various temperatures and timeframes. This ensures that the last spray in the bottle is just as effective as the first.
