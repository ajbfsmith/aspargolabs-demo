I’m 28, and I had erectile dysfunction (ED) for two years before I finally worked up the courage to ask for help. 

For a long time, I thought I was the only person my age dealing with this. We’re conditioned to believe that ED is something that only happens to men in their 60s or 70s. When it started happening to me in my mid-20s, I didn't just feel frustrated; I felt broken. I spent two years making excuses, avoiding intimacy, and spiraling into a cycle of performance anxiety that only made the physical symptoms worse.

If you are in your 20s or 30s and struggling, you should know that you aren't an anomaly. Research published in the [Journal of Sexual Medicine](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23651423/) suggests that as many as 1 in 4 men seeking help for new-onset ED are under the age of 40. 

## The Two-Year Silence

My journey started with a few "off" nights that I blamed on stress or having one too many drinks. But soon, the occasional lapse became a pattern. I’m 28, I had a great job, a healthy social life, and a partner I cared about, but my body wasn't cooperating. 

Instead of seeking medical advice, I went internal. I started "head-checking"—constantly monitoring my arousal during intimate moments. This created a feedback loop of anxiety. The more I worried about failing, the more my sympathetic nervous system (the "fight or flight" response) took over, making an erection physiologically impossible.

This silence took a toll on my partner, too. Without a clear medical explanation, she began to wonder if the problem was her or if I was losing interest. I realized that [erectile dysfunction by age](https://acceleratehealth.com/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s) varies significantly in its causes. This realization—that my ED was a medical symptom rather than a character flaw—finally pushed me to see a clinician.

## Important Safety Information

Before starting any treatment for ED, it is vital to consult with a healthcare provider. PDE5 inhibitors like sildenafil (the active ingredient in HEZKUE) are not safe for everyone. 

**Do not take HEZKUE if you:**
- Take nitrates (often prescribed for chest pain).
- Use "poppers" (amyl nitrite).
- Have unstable cardiovascular disease or have been advised by a doctor to avoid sexual activity due to heart risks.

Seek urgent medical care if you experience chest pain, symptoms of a heart attack, sudden vision or hearing loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
