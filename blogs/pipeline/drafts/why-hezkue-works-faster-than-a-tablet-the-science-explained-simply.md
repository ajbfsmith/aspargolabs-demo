## The Journey of a Tablet: Why Pills Take Time

To understand why **hezkue works faster**, we first have to look at the path a traditional tablet must take. When you swallow a pill, it doesn't enter the bloodstream immediately. It must travel through the esophagus and into the stomach, where it begins the process of disintegration.

1.  **Disintegration:** The solid pill must break down into smaller chunks.
2.  **Dissolution:** Those chunks must dissolve into a liquid solution that the body can actually absorb.
3.  **Gastric Emptying:** The dissolved medication must pass from the stomach into the small intestine, which is the primary site of absorption for most oral drugs.
4.  **First-Pass Metabolism:** Once absorbed by the intestines, the drug travels through the portal vein to the liver. The liver filters the blood, often breaking down a significant portion of the medication before it ever reaches the rest of the body. This is known as the "first-pass effect."

According to the [National Library of Medicine](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12126221/), sildenafil tablets typically reach peak plasma concentrations within 30 to 120 minutes. This variability is one reason why the [complete guide erectile dysfunction](https://acceleratehealth.com/blog/complete-guide-erectile-dysfunction) emphasizes planning ahead when using traditional oral medications.
