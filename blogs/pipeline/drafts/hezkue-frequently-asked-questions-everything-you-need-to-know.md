## How does HEZKUE work?

HEZKUE belongs to a class of medications known as phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) inhibitors. According to the [National Institutes of Health](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK526043/), PDE5 inhibitors work by enhancing the effects of nitric oxide, a natural chemical the body produces to relax muscles in the penis. This relaxation increases blood flow, allowing for an erection in response to sexual stimulation.

The innovation of HEZKUE lies in its suspension format. By delivering sildenafil as a fine mist, the medication is designed for efficient administration. It is important to note that HEZKUE is not a "miracle cure" or an aphrodisiac; it requires sexual arousal to be effective.

## How is an oral spray different from a pill?

The primary difference between HEZKUE and traditional ED pills is the delivery mechanism. When you swallow a tablet, it must undergo "first-pass metabolism," where the stomach and liver process the medication before it enters the bloodstream.

Research published in [the Journal of Sexual Medicine](https://academic.oup.com/jsm/article/13/12/1932/6963499) suggests that oral spray formulations of sildenafil can offer a different user experience regarding onset and convenience. Because the suspension is already liquid, it removes the need for the body to break down a solid pill, which can lead to more consistent results regardless of whether you have recently eaten.

## Is HEZKUE safe for everyone?

No medication is safe for everyone, and HEZKUE is no exception. It is a potent clinical treatment that must be used under medical supervision.

**Important Safety Warning:** You must not take HEZKUE if you use nitrates (often prescribed for chest pain) or guanylate cyclase stimulators (for pulmonary hypertension). The combination can cause a sudden, life-threatening drop in blood pressure.

Men with unstable cardiovascular disease, a recent history of heart attack or stroke, or severe liver or kidney impairment should speak with a clinician before considering HEZKUE. If you experience chest pain, sudden vision loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism), seek emergency medical care immediately.
