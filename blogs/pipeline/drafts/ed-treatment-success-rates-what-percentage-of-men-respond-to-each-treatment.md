When a man begins his journey toward treating erectile dysfunction (ED), the first question is almost always: "Will this actually work for me?" Because ED is a complex condition involving blood flow, nerve signals, and psychological factors, no single treatment has a 100% success rate. However, modern medicine has reached a point where the vast majority of men can find a successful solution.

Understanding **ed treatment success rates** requires looking past the marketing and into the clinical data. Success is generally measured by the ability to achieve and maintain an erection sufficient for sexual intercourse. Here is how the most common treatments compare.

## Success Rates of PDE5 Inhibitors (Oral Medications)

Phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) inhibitors, such as sildenafil and tadalafil, are the first-line treatments for ED. For many, these medications are the gold standard because of their ease of use and non-invasive nature.

- **General Success Rates:** Clinical data from the [American Urological Association](https://www.auanet.org/guidelines-and-quality/guidelines/erectile-dysfunction-guideline) suggests that PDE5 inhibitors are effective for approximately 60% to 80% of men.
- **Sildenafil:** This is the active ingredient in many ED treatments, including HEZKUE. In broad clinical trials, sildenafil has consistently shown an efficacy rate of about 70% to 82% depending on the dosage.
- **Tadalafil:** Known for its long duration, tadalafil shows similar success rates, with roughly 70% to 80% of men reporting improved erections.

While these numbers are high, they are not universal. Success often depends on the underlying cause of the ED. For example, men with severe nerve damage from prostate surgery or advanced diabetes may find that oral medications are less effective than they are for men with mild vascular issues.

## Success Rates of Second-Line and Third-Line Treatments

When oral medications do not provide the desired results, clinicians often turn to more direct delivery methods or mechanical aids.

- **Intracavernosal Injections (ICI):** These involve injecting medication directly into the side of the penis. According to the [Urology Care Foundation](https://www.urologyhealth.org/urology-a-z/e/erectile-dysfunction-(ed)), injections have a success rate of 85% to 90%, making them one of the most effective options for men who do not respond to pills.
- **Vacuum Erection Devices (VED):** These mechanical pumps use suction to draw blood into the penis. While they have a high "mechanical" success rate (often cited above 90%), long-term user satisfaction is sometimes lower due to the lack of spontaneity and the need for a constriction ring.
- **Penile Implants:** For men with severe ED that does not respond to any other treatment, surgical implants are an option. The [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/tests-procedures/penile-implant/about/pac-20384917) reports that satisfaction rates for penile implants are exceptionally high, with over 90% of patients and their partners reporting positive outcomes.

## Factors That Affect ED Treatment Success

The effectiveness of any treatment is rarely a "yes or no" outcome. Several variables influence how well a man responds to a specific therapy.

### Underlying Health Conditions
Success rates are often lower in men with specific comorbidities. For instance, men with poorly managed diabetes may see success rates with oral PDE5 inhibitors drop to around 50% to 60%. This is because diabetes can cause both vascular damage and neuropathy, making it harder for the medication to facilitate the necessary chemical signals for an erection.

### Age and Severity
While ED is not an inevitable part of aging, [erectile dysfunction by age](/blog/ed-causes-by-age-20s-30s-40s-50s-60s) is a significant factor. Younger men often respond more robustly to lower doses of medication, while older men may require higher doses or different delivery formats to achieve the same result.

### Psychological Factors
If ED is primarily driven by performance anxiety or stress, medication can help bridge the gap, but the "success" may be inconsistent unless the underlying psychological triggers are addressed.

## Safety and Medical Considerations

It is vital to remember that while **ed treatment success** is high, these treatments are medical interventions that require professional guidance.

**Important Safety Information:**
- PDE5 inhibitors (including sildenafil and tadalafil) are strictly contraindicated for men taking nitrates for chest pain or heart conditions. Combining these can cause a life-threatening drop in blood pressure.
- Men with unstable cardiovascular disease or those who have been advised against sexual activity due to heart health must speak with a clinician before seeking treatment.
- Seek emergency medical care immediately if you experience chest pain during sex, sudden vision or hearing loss, or an erection lasting longer than four hours (priapism).
