## What Makes HEZKUE Different?

HEZKUE delivers sildenafil via an oral spray rather than a traditional solid tablet. This delivery format is designed to change the user experience by offering a more portable and discreet option. Despite the innovative delivery method, the underlying pharmacology remains the same: it is a PDE5 inhibitor that works by relaxing the blood vessels in the penis to allow for increased blood flow during sexual stimulation.

## Common Side Effects of Sildenafil

Because HEZKUE contains sildenafil, users may experience side effects similar to those associated with other PDE5 inhibitors. According to the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/drugs-supplements/sildenafil-oral-route/side-effects/drg-20066904), most side effects are mild to moderate and often diminish as the medication wears off.

Commonly reported side effects include:

- **Headaches:** This is the most frequently reported side effect due to the way the medication dilates blood vessels.
- **Flushing:** A feeling of warmth or redness in the face, neck, or chest.
- **Dyspepsia:** Indigestion or an upset stomach.
- **Nasal Congestion:** A "stuffy" nose resulting from dilated blood vessels in the nasal passages.
- **Visual Changes:** Some men report a blue tint to their vision or increased sensitivity to light.
- **Back Pain or Muscle Aches:** These typically occur several hours after use and resolve within a day or two.

## Who Should Not Use HEZKUE: Contraindications

Safety is paramount when using PDE5 inhibitors. There are specific medical conditions and drug interactions that make HEZKUE unsafe for certain individuals.

### Nitrates and Blood Pressure
The most critical contraindication involves nitrates. Men taking nitrates for chest pain (angina) or heart problems must never use HEZKUE. Combining sildenafil with nitrates can cause a sudden, life-threatening drop in blood pressure. This also applies to recreational "poppers" (amyl nitrate or butyl nitrate).

### Cardiovascular Health
Men with unstable cardiovascular disease, a recent history of heart attack or stroke (within the last six months), or severe heart failure should not use HEZKUE without explicit clearance from a cardiologist. Sexual activity itself can place a strain on the heart, and [Harvard Health](https://www.health.harvard.edu/mens-health/are-erectile-dysfunction-pills-safe-for-men-with-heart-disease) notes that those with significant heart risks must be evaluated by a clinician before starting ED treatment.

### Other Medical Conditions
You should speak with a clinician if you have:
- Severe liver or kidney disease.
- Retinitis pigmentosa (a rare genetic eye disease).
- A history of non-arteritic anterior ischemic optic neuropathy (NAION).
- Anatomical deformations of the penis (such as Peyronie's disease).

## Serious Safety Warnings

While rare, some side effects require immediate medical intervention. If you experience any of the following while using HEZKUE, seek urgent medical care:

1. **Priapism:** An erection that lasts longer than four hours. If not treated promptly, this can cause permanent damage to the penile tissue.
2. **Sudden Vision Loss:** A sudden decrease or loss of vision in one or both eyes.
3. **Sudden Hearing Loss:** A sudden decrease or loss of hearing, sometimes accompanied by ringing in the ears (tinnitus) or dizziness.
4. **Chest Pain or Symptoms of a Heart Attack:** Including pressure in the chest, shortness of breath, or pain radiating to the jaw or arm.
